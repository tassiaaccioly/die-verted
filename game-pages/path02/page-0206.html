You wake up and it seems like the extra five minutes didn't do any good for your life. You wonder to yourself how can five minutes make any difference? You feel like crap, maybe if you slept more you would be rested, but you don't have time for that anymore.

You change clothes fast and get out of the apartment. You know that extra five minutes will translate in you getting to work right on time. Security at your building's gate is doing a scheduled routine search with everyone entering or leaving the premises. You remember now seeing the notice that had been showing on your apartment console and regret not waking up 5 minutes earlier. This will probably make you later than you expected. You will probably need to work another late shift to cover for the lateness. You enter the line to get some breakfast rations at some shady half door, commissary style, where a weirdly not tattoed genderless human without any hair on his body delivers ration chips against his will.

As you wait in the long line, you receive a connection. It's your father, they want to know if you're ok. They say something about a weird event and seeing rocks and dirt. You can't quite understand them, but you don't really need to as the screen above the commissary starts blinking in red with urgent news.

The screen shows a high view footage from the place where the police precinct was and that now shows a very large hole in the ground with about a kilometer diameter. Some authorities and policepeople surround the place the camera is showing. Now you understand what your mother was saying about rocks and dirt. The hole is very deep and has crossed the titanium cover on the earth, and for the first time in your life you can see dirt and rocks in it. And you finally realize:

There's no more precinct. No more work. No more case. No more game.
