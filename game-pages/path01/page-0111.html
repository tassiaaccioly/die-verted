Kirlian Krizik: I had to kill the ship. There was something wrong with ROS-E5. It kept accusing errors and malfunctions. Everybody started accusing each other for the malfunctions and everyone grew suspicious of each other, but at one time we realised it was the computer. ROS-E5 was accusing malfunctions and errors that specifically seemed like things someone could be doing. It has a low chance of malfunction but it could only be it. So our only option was to kill the navigational computer.

But for that we would have to disconnect the whole ship and we would not have enough air for everybody to survive the whole way without engines. We picked straws and my friend was the one to get the surviving straw.
